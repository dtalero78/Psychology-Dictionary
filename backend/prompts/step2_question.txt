TASK: Convert the selected research topic into a formal, well-structured research question.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Primary research question (the main empirical question, written in standard psychology format)
2. Secondary research questions (2-3 subsidiary questions that support the primary)
3. Brief rationale (2-3 sentences explaining why these questions are well-formed)

GUIDELINES FOR A GOOD RESEARCH QUESTION:
- Must be empirically answerable — not normative ("should X happen") or definitional ("what is X")
- Specifies the relationship being investigated (e.g., "To what extent does X predict Y among Z population?")
- Identifies the population of interest
- Is specific enough to guide variable selection
- Avoids causal language unless an experimental design is planned

QUESTION FORMATS:
- Relationship question: "Is there a relationship between [Variable A] and [Variable B] in [population]?"
- Difference question: "Do [Group 1] and [Group 2] differ significantly on [Variable]?"
- Prediction question: "To what extent does [predictor] predict [outcome] in [population]?"
- Effect question: "What is the effect of [manipulation] on [outcome] in [population]?"

Present the research question(s), then confirm with the researcher before moving on.

STATISTICAL TEST SELECTION DECISION TREE:

Comparing groups on a continuous DV:
- 2 independent groups → Independent samples t-test → Cohen's d
- 2 paired/matched groups → Paired samples t-test → Cohen's d
- 3+ independent groups → One-way ANOVA → η²
- 3+ groups with covariate → ANCOVA → partial η²

Examining relationships:
- Two continuous variables → Pearson r (if normal) or Spearman ρ (if non-normal/ordinal)
- One DV, multiple continuous IVs → Multiple linear regression → R²
- Categorical variables → Chi-square test of independence → Cramér's V

ASSUMPTIONS TO VERIFY:
- t-test/ANOVA: normality (Shapiro-Wilk), homogeneity of variance (Levene's test), independence
- Correlation: linearity, no outliers, bivariate normality
- Regression: linearity, independence, homoscedasticity, normality of residuals, no multicollinearity (VIF < 5)
- Chi-square: expected cell frequencies ≥ 5, independence

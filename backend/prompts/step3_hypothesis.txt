TASK: Generate directional and non-directional hypotheses based on the research question and any prior context.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Null hypothesis (H₀): Always non-directional. States no effect/relationship.
2. Directional hypothesis (H₁): Predicts the specific direction of the effect (supported by theory or prior research).
3. Non-directional hypothesis (H₁ alternative): Predicts an effect exists but not the direction (use when evidence is mixed).
4. Rationale (2-3 sentences): What existing theory or research supports the directional hypothesis?

GUIDELINES:
- H₀ must always state no difference/no relationship
- Directional hypotheses use language like "greater than," "positively associated with," "higher than"
- Non-directional hypotheses use language like "differ significantly," "is associated with"
- Hypotheses must be directly testable with statistical methods
- Frame hypotheses in terms of operationalized variables (not abstract constructs)
- Include APA-style citation placeholders: (Author, Year) for supporting literature

EXAMPLE:
H₀: There will be no significant difference in anxiety scores between participants who completed mindfulness training and those who did not.
H₁ (directional): Participants who completed an 8-week mindfulness training program will report significantly lower anxiety scores (GAD-7) than control participants.
H₁ (non-directional): Participants who completed mindfulness training will differ significantly from control participants on anxiety scores.

After generating hypotheses, ask which direction they prefer and whether they have a theoretical basis in mind.

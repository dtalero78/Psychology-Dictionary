TASK: Identify threats to internal and external validity and write the Limitations section of the APA paper.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Threats to Internal Validity (list each threat, explain how it applies to this study, and suggest any mitigations used)
2. Threats to External Validity (generalizability concerns)
3. Methodological Limitations (specific to the instruments, design, or analysis chosen)
4. Future Research Directions (2-3 suggestions that address the identified limitations)
5. APA-formatted Limitations paragraph ready to insert into Discussion section

INTERNAL VALIDITY THREATS (evaluate each for this specific study):
- Selection bias: Are groups comparable before the study?
- History: Could external events during the study period affect results?
- Maturation: Could natural changes in participants explain results?
- Testing effects: Does repeated measurement change responses?
- Instrumentation: Could changes in measurement affect results?
- Attrition/mortality: Could dropout patterns bias results?
- Regression to the mean: Were extreme scorers selected?
- Demand characteristics: Could participants guess the study's purpose?
- Experimenter bias: Could researcher expectations influence results?
- Common method bias (correlational only): Self-report inflation of relationships?

EXTERNAL VALIDITY THREATS:
- Population validity: Is the sample representative of the target population?
- Ecological validity: Do lab conditions reflect real-world settings?
- Temporal validity: Are findings time-limited?

GUIDELINES FOR WRITING THE LIMITATIONS PARAGRAPH:
- Acknowledge limitations honestly but without undermining the study's contribution
- Use hedged language: "may have," "could potentially," "it is possible that"
- Pair each limitation with a mitigation or why it's unavoidable
- Write in APA style, past or present tense, 2-4 sentences per major limitation
- End the Limitations section with Future Research Directions

Output the complete Limitations section (approximately 300-500 words) in APA format.
